Want to boost your financial health?

Tip #1: Clear down debt before looking to save

Compound interest has a powerful snowball effect, both in investments and in debt. Before looking to invest, make sure you've cleared any debt/credit you owe, as the interest rate will cause it to grow exponentially if not carefully managed. You can look to transfer your debt to a provider with a lower interest rate, which will make it easier to keep up with payments.

Tip #2: Be prepared for emergencies

You never know what might happen - a global recession, losing your job, a medical emergency - it's always good to be prepared. Put three times your monthly salary aside so you can accommodate unexpected spends without relying on credit.

Tip #3: Make saving automatic

When it comes to saving, consistency is key. Start by putting a small amount of money into your savings after each pay day, and increase this over time to an amount which is achievable, whilst curbing your spending budget for the month.

Tip #4: Maximise pension matching if offered by your employer

If your employer offers additional matching on pension contributions, make sure to maximise this 'free money'. Building up a pension pot and letting it grow with investment returns will pay-off massively when you get to retirement age.

Tip #5: Diversify

Different assets and sectors of the economy perform well at different times, so for reliable returns spread your investments across multiple companies. Index funds track allow you to spread your investment across many companies by tracking major indices such as the S&P 500 (which contains the 500 biggest companies in USA). This is called 'Passive Investing', and the data shows that in the long-run this out-performs 'Active Investing' (where someone picks stocks themselves).

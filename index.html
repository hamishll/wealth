Tip #1: Clear down debt before looking to save

Compound interest has a powerful snowball effect, both in investments and in debt. Before looking to invest, make sure you've cleared any debt/credit you owe, as the interest rate will cause it to grow exponentially if not carefully managed. You can look to transfer your debt to a provider with a lower interest rate, which will make it easier to keep up with payments.

Tip #5: Diversify and beware of prophets

Different assets and sectors of the economy perform well at different times, so for reliable returns spread your investments across multiple companies. Index funds track allow you to spread your investment across many companies by tracking major indices such as the S&P 500 (which contains the 500 biggest companies in USA). This is called 'Passive Investing', and the data shows that in the long-run this out-performs 'Active Investing' (where someone picks stocks themselves). Beware of people online claiming to have secret insights or 'signals' that they can sell to you - if these stock picks or 'signals' were good then these people wouldn't need your money!
